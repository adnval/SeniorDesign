# Individual Capstone Assessment - Kate Schmidlin  

&emsp; &ensp; Our Senior Design project is a social media platform that allows people to share art that they 
discover, or visit/view art that others have discovered, using the assistance and amplified by the 
experience of AR technology. A user should be able to be directed through taking a series of 
photos of art they discovered, which is compiled into an AR experience of that art, and is then 
posted. Other users then have the opportunity to interact with that art. Imagine being able to see 
cool street art in Pakistan or Brazil from the comfort of your home. On the other hand, we want 
this platform to inspire connection. I suggested taking inspiration from the very popular 
“Pokemon GO”, and creating some sort of award system for visiting or posting art. This 
encourages people to not only view online, but to go enjoy this art and support these artists in 
person. With our big-ideas brainstorming complete, I am very encouraged with how our project 
has come together.   

&emsp; &ensp; I would say that my Software Engineering course was the primary influence on how I approach 
this project. That was my 2nd experience after ENED working really in depth on 1 project with 
the same team for an entire semester. These experiences really shaped not only how I approach 
this project, but how I have approached many co-ops and personal projects since. I have found I 
really enjoy being able to plan out each step, and hitting those milestones, even when they don’t 
come together exactly as expected. This experience, as well as experiencing the lack of 
management during ENED, gave me the skill set and personal drive, to be really strict with time 
management. It has always paid off in the long run, and as someone who desires to enter a 
managerial role at some point in my career, I am excited to have more opportunities to exercise 
this muscle.   

&emsp; &ensp; During a Co-op rotation I had the pleasure of working with another co-op to complete a product 
of high company value over the course of a semester. This project, similarly to the project we 
selected for Senior Design, was one that going into I had very limited exposure to the skill set 
necessary to complete. For Senior Design I will need to work with Augmented Reality, which I 
have never worked with on the production side. However, my co-op experience taught me how 
to balance learning new skills on the spot, as well as being very critical of deliverables when you 
aren’t 100% familiar with what you are producing. I am excited to learn something new, and 
honest with myself about the challenges this will likely also cause. In order to counteract any 
expected setbacks, I have learned we will need to move our milestones up about a month for a 
project of this scale.   

&emsp; &ensp; A big motivator for me on this project is where I see my career going, and how that aligns with 
my personal goals. It is apparent to me, my peers, and society, that the new AI, VR, and AR 
technologies will be the future of the computer science world. With that said, I  have very little 
technical experience with either. So I am very appreciative of the opportunity to dive into AR 
software from that perspective. I also personally take a lot of enjoyment from VR and AR 
softwares. They are such a fun collaboration between what sits all around us, and what is 
normally confined to a screen. I view it as an outlet of pure creativity, and could not think of a 
project that would suit my interests better.   

&emsp; &ensp; To approach this I am looking into AR software current developments, and what could be 
applicable to our project. I intend to create this on a small scale with 1 or 2 images projections, 
prioritizing user friendly-ness over excessively indepth AR technological capabilities. I am 
expecting that we will be able to instruct a user through a series of photos that enables a movable 
view of art throughout the city. I will know we are done when multiple test users are able to 
engage with each other's posted art AR uploads. Ultimately, this design is meant to inspire 
connection between everyday people, artists, and enthusiasts alike. As a member of my team, I 
am invested in ensuring that mission comes to fruition, and that I have made substantial 
contributions to the process. If my peers feel supported by me (and I feel supported by them), if 
our milestones are always met, and the users have gained better access to the people and art 
around them, I will feel our project is complete.
